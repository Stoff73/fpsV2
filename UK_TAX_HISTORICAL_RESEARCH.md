## Income Tax

### Personal Allowance
**Frozen across all 5 years**: £12,570

- 2021/22: £12,570
- 2022/23: £12,570
- 2023/24: £12,570
- 2024/25: £12,570
- 2025/26: £12,570

**Note**: Chancellor Rishi Sunak froze the personal allowance in Spring 2021 Budget through 2025/26. Extended to April 2028 in Autumn Statement 2022.

### Income Tax Rates
All 5 years have the same rates:
- **Basic rate**: 20%
- **Higher rate**: 40%
- **Additional rate**: 45%

### Income Tax Bands

**2021/22:**
- Personal Allowance: £0 - £12,570 (0%)
- Basic rate: £12,571 - £50,270 (20%)
- Higher rate: £50,271 - £150,000 (40%)
- Additional rate: Over £150,000 (45%)

**2022/23:**
- Personal Allowance: £0 - £12,570 (0%)
- Basic rate: £12,571 - £50,270 (20%)
- Higher rate: £50,271 - £150,000 (40%)
- Additional rate: Over £150,000 (45%)

**2023/24, 2024/25, 2025/26:**
- Personal Allowance: £0 - £12,570 (0%)
- Basic rate: £12,571 - £50,270 (20%)
- Higher rate: £50,271 - £125,140 (40%)
- Additional rate: Over £125,140 (45%)

**Key Change**: Additional rate threshold reduced from £150,000 to £125,140 from 6 April 2023.

## National Insurance

### Class 1 (Employee) Contributions

**2021/22:**
- Primary Threshold: £9,568/year (£184/week)
- Upper Earnings Limit: £50,270/year
- Rate (up to UEL): 12%
- Rate (above UEL): 2%

**2022/23 (Complex year with multiple changes):**
- **April 2022**: 1.25% increase introduced - rates became 13.25% / 3.25%
- **July 2022**: Primary Threshold increased to £12,570 (aligned with personal allowance)
- **November 2022**: 1.25% increase reversed - rates returned to 12% / 2%

**2023/24:**
- Primary Threshold: £12,570/year
- Upper Earnings Limit: £50,270/year
- Rate (up to UEL): 12% (until Jan 2024), then 10% (from 6 Jan 2024)
- Rate (above UEL): 2%

**2024/25:**
- Primary Threshold: £12,570/year
- Upper Earnings Limit: £50,270/year
- Rate (up to UEL): 10% (until April), then 8% (from April 2024)
- Rate (above UEL): 2%

**2025/26:**
- Primary Threshold: £12,570/year
- Upper Earnings Limit: £50,270/year
- Rate (up to UEL): 8%
- Rate (above UEL): 2%

### Class 1 (Employer) Contributions

**All years 2021/22 through 2025/26:**
- Secondary Threshold: £9,100/year (2021-22), £9,100/year (2022-23), £9,100/year (2023-24+)
- Rate above threshold: 13.8%

**Note**: Employer rates also had 1.25% increase in 2022-23 (temporarily), reverted Nov 2022.

### Class 2 (Self-Employed) Contributions

**2021/22 through 2023/24:**
- Small Profits Threshold: £6,515 (2021-22), £6,725 (2022-23), £6,725 (2023-24)
- Flat weekly rate: £3.05 (2021-22), £3.15 (2022-23), £3.45 (2023-24)

**2024/25 and 2025/26:**
- **Abolished** from April 2024 for self-employed with profits above lower profits limit

### Class 4 (Self-Employed) Contributions

**2021/22:**
- Lower Profits Limit: £9,568
- Upper Profits Limit: £50,270
- Rate (between limits): 9%
- Rate (above upper limit): 2%

**2022/23:**
- Lower Profits Limit: £9,880 (April-June), then £11,908 (July-March)
- Upper Profits Limit: £50,270
- Rate (between limits): 10.25% (April-Oct), then 9% (Nov-March)
- Rate (above upper limit): 3.25% (April-Oct), then 2% (Nov-March)

**2023/24:**
- Lower Profits Limit: £12,570
- Upper Profits Limit: £50,270
- Rate (between limits): 9%
- Rate (above upper limit): 2%

**2024/25 and 2025/26:**
- Lower Profits Limit: £12,570
- Upper Profits Limit: £50,270
- Rate (between limits): 6% (from April 2024)
- Rate (above upper limit): 2%

## Pensions

### Annual Allowance

**2021/22 and 2022/23**: £40,000
**2023/24, 2024/25, 2025/26**: £60,000

**Key Change**: Annual allowance increased from £40,000 to £60,000 from 6 April 2023.

### Money Purchase Annual Allowance (MPAA)

**2021/22 and 2022/23**: £4,000
**2023/24, 2024/25, 2025/26**: £10,000

**Triggered when**: Flexibly accessing pension benefits.

### Tapered Annual Allowance

**All years (2021/22 through 2025/26)**:
- Threshold Income: £200,000
- Adjusted Income Threshold: £260,000 (2021-22 onwards, was £240,000 before)
- Taper: £1 reduction for every £2 over threshold
- Minimum Tapered Allowance:
  - 2021/22 & 2022/23: £4,000
  - 2023/24 onwards: £10,000

### Lifetime Allowance

**2021/22 and 2022/23**: £1,073,100
**2023/24**: Effectively abolished (no tax charge)
**2024/25 onwards**: Completely abolished

### State Pension (Full New State Pension)

## Inheritance Tax (IHT)

### Nil Rate Band (NRB)
**Frozen across all 5 years**: £325,000

- 2021/22: £325,000
- 2022/23: £325,000
- 2023/24: £325,000
- 2024/25: £325,000
- 2025/26: £325,000

**Note**: NRB has been frozen at £325,000 since 2009/10. Extended freeze through to April 2030.

### Residence Nil Rate Band (RNRB)
**Frozen across all 5 years**: £175,000

- 2021/22: £175,000
- 2022/23: £175,000
- 2023/24: £175,000
- 2024/25: £175,000
- 2025/26: £175,000

**Note**: RNRB reached maximum of £175,000 in 2020/21 and has been frozen since.

### RNRB Taper Threshold
**Frozen across all 5 years**: £2,000,000

Estate value above which RNRB is tapered away at £1 for every £2 over threshold.

### IHT Rate
**Unchanged**: 40% on amount above NRB + RNRB

### Reduced Rate for Charity
**Unchanged**: 36% if 10% or more of net estate left to charity

### Gifting Exemptions

**Annual Exemption**: £3,000 per year (all years)
**Small Gifts**: £250 per person per year (all years)
**Wedding/Civil Partnership Gifts**:
- From parent: £5,000
- From grandparent: £2,500
- From others: £1,000

**Regular Gifts from Income**: Unlimited (if from surplus income)

### PET (Potentially Exempt Transfer) Taper Relief

7-year rule with taper relief:
- 0-3 years before death: 40% tax
- 3-4 years: 32% (20% relief)
- 4-5 years: 24% (40% relief)
- 5-6 years: 16% (60% relief)
- 6-7 years: 8% (80% relief)
- 7+ years: 0% (100% relief)

### CLT (Chargeable Lifetime Transfer)

- Lifetime rate: 20% (on amount above available NRB)
- Death rate: 40% (if donor dies within 7 years)
- Lookback period: 7 years for cumulation

## Stamp Duty Land Tax (SDLT)

**Complex with multiple rate periods:**

### Period 1: 1 October 2021 - 22 September 2022
Covers most of 2021/22 and early 2022/23

**Residential - Standard**:
- £0 - £125,000: 0%
- £125,001 - £250,000: 2%
- £250,001 - £925,000: 5%
- £925,001 - £1,500,000: 10%
- Over £1,500,000: 12%

**Residential - Additional Properties** (add 3% to all bands):
- £0 - £125,000: 3%
- £125,001 - £250,000: 5%
- £250,001 - £925,000: 8%
- £925,001 - £1,500,000: 13%
- Over £1,500,000: 15%

**First Time Buyers**:
- £0 - £300,000: 0%
- £300,001 - £500,000: 5%
- Relief not available if property over £500,000

### Period 2: 23 September 2022 - 30 October 2024
Covers most of 2022/23, all of 2023/24, and most of 2024/25

**Residential - Standard**:
- £0 - £250,000: 0% **(threshold increased)**
- £250,001 - £925,000: 5%
- £925,001 - £1,500,000: 10%
- Over £1,500,000: 12%

**Residential - Additional Properties** (add 3% to all bands):
- £0 - £250,000: 3%
- £250,001 - £925,000: 8%
- £925,001 - £1,500,000: 13%
- Over £1,500,000: 15%

**First Time Buyers**:
- £0 - £425,000: 0% **(threshold increased from £300k)**
- £425,001 - £625,000: 5%
- Relief not available if property over £625,000 **(increased from £500k)**

### Period 3: 31 October 2024 - 31 March 2025
Rest of 2024/25

**Residential - Standard** (same as Period 2):
- £0 - £250,000: 0%
- £250,001 - £925,000: 5%
- £925,001 - £1,500,000: 10%

**First Time Buyers** (same as Period 2):
- £0 - £425,000: 0%

- Relief not available if property over £625,000

### Period 4: From 1 April 2025

- £125,001 - £250,000: 2%

## Summary of Major Changes

### 2022/23
- Dividend allowance unchanged at £2,000, but rates increased by 1.25pp
- NI rates had multiple changes (1.25% rise, then reversed)
- SDLT threshold increased from £125k to £250k (Sept 2022)
- First time buyer SDLT relief increased

### 2023/24
- Additional rate income tax threshold reduced from £150k to £125,140
- Dividend allowance halved from £2,000 to £1,000
- CGT annual exemption halved from £12,300 to £6,000
- Pension annual allowance increased from £40k to £60k
- MPAA increased from £4k to £10k
- Lifetime allowance abolished (no tax charge)

### 2024/25
- Dividend allowance halved again from £1,000 to £500
- CGT annual exemption halved again from £6,000 to £3,000
- CGT rates increased (Oct 2024): 10%/20% → 18%/24%
- Employee NI reduced from 12% to 10% (Jan), then to 8% (April)
- Class 4 NI reduced from 9% to 6% (April)
- Class 2 NI abolished (April)
- SDLT additional dwelling surcharge increased from 3% to 5% (Oct)

### 2025/26
- SDLT standard threshold reverts from £250k to £125k (April)
- First time buyer relief reverts: £425k → £300k, max £625k → £500k
- All other rates remain frozen
